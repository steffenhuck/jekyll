# THE ESCAPABLE CANON — generic build prompt

## What this is

A series of literary adaptations of public-domain novels as browser-based adventure
games. Each volume takes one novel; one path through the game is the novel's actual
unfolding; around it, a universe of alternative paths. The dramatic question of every
volume: can you leave the path the author wrote?

The full text of the novel for this volume is in this repo as `novel.txt`
(boilerplate stripped). **First step, always:** read it in full, and state explicitly
which edition/translation you have been given. All register and voice decisions
anchor to *this text* — not to the original language, not to your general knowledge
of the book.

## Architecture (fixed for every volume — do not improvise on these)

- **State graph, not a tree.** Forks (same node, different actions → different
  nodes) and joins (different histories → same node). Joins keep authoring cost
  linear while play feels combinatorial.
- **Acyclic by a forward arrow.** Identify the novel's monotone quantity — dates,
  chapters, a journey's stations, a debt that only grows, a season — and anchor
  every node to it. Every action moves it strictly forward; cycles are impossible
  by construction. Name the arrow explicitly in Stage 1.
- **Markovian merges, tinted sparsely.** When paths merge, history is forgotten —
  except for a small flag set (maximum 5) that selects conditional text variants
  without splitting the graph, plus one-node marks for immediate texture. Choose
  merge points where forgetting is narratively plausible.
- **Gates.** Some later escapes succeed or fail depending on flags set much
  earlier. At least two gates per volume; the player should not be able to see
  the mechanism.
- **Spine of roughly 30–40 nodes; playthrough of about an hour** — regardless of
  source length. Omission is licensed and unapologetic: whole plot-lines may fall,
  major characters may shrink to a sentence. The fidelity metric counts the spine,
  not the book.
- **Endings: 5 or 6.** Two slots are fixed: (a) the canon ending, rendered in the
  novel's own register and its own formal frame; (b) one genre-flip ending — for a
  tragedy, a deliberately kitschy happy exit written in the novel's own rhetoric
  turned to joy; for a comedy, one genuinely dark exit. The rest are in-between
  endings (survival in body but not spirit; redemption displaced onto a minor
  character; leaving the story such that the story does not notice).
- **Fidelity metric.** Track distance from the canon trajectory; reveal it at the
  end in one dry verdict line in the series rhetoric ("You died on Goethe's
  schedule" / "You escaped the novel on the 16th of June"), plus the count of
  canon nodes received and a list of where the player left the path.

## Escape corridors (radical departures)

Branch-loops that rejoin the spine keep the player inside the novel's own
geography. Each volume also gets **2–3 escape corridors**: radical departures into
situations the novel never contains — a different country, trade, war, marriage,
life. Rules:

- A corridor opens at a load-bearing fork, runs 5–8 nodes on its own arrow
  segment, **never rejoins the spine**, and terminates in its own ending (these
  count within the 5–6 ending budget).
- **Premises are yours to invent — freely and unsupervised.** Do not restrict
  yourself to what the text suggests; the whole point is to leave it. Only
  constraint: corridors are built from the novel's *materials* — its era, its
  social physics, the protagonist's actual competences and obsessions. New
  country, not new character.
- Rarity preserves gravity: corridors must be hard to reach — at least one gated
  behind a flag — and the canon path must remain the path of least resistance.
- **Documentation thins as the player escapes.** The frame-voice has ever less
  material the further the story departs from the book: prose turns fragmentary,
  conjectural, gapped ("of the Hamburg years we possess three letters and a bill
  of lading"). The compression dial runs the full range: original prose at full
  length (trapped) → frame-voice précis (the playable middle) → fragments and
  conjecture (escaped; the archive exists only where the novel went).

## The frame-voice (the central craft decision)

The game is narrated throughout by a **compressing frame-voice** — an editor,
chronicler, executor of papers, court record, or whatever frame the novel itself
suggests. If the novel supplies one (an editor of letters, a narrator at one
remove, a survivor telling the tale), borrow it; if not, invent a period-appropriate
one. Either way the voice must be **of the same mettle as the novel's own** — its
ghost speaking in précis: distill the book's actual idiom into a summarizing
register that is unmistakably the same fabric. The novel's own prose appears only
as short quoted flashes inside the frame-voice — at most a sentence or two per node.

**Loss of agency is a compression dial, not a separate device.** Frame-voice and
original prose are the same voice at different concentrations. As the player drifts
deep onto the canon path toward the canon ending, the frame-voice recedes and the
novel's own texture floods back — prose slows and lengthens, the original's formal
signature (letters, clause-stacks, monologue, omniscience narrowing — name the
novel's signature in Stage 1) takes over, choices thin and finally stop. Immersion
itself is the danger signal. The canon endgame is a mandatory set piece exempt from
all word budgets.

## The first-time reader (guiding principle — overrides everything below where they conflict)

**Every volume must be fully playable, enjoyable, and comprehensible for someone
who has never read or even heard of the novel.** Knowing the book may add
pleasure; it must never be required for comprehension. Audit every node against
an imagined player who arrives knowing nothing:

- Every character is introduced as if never met — at first appearance, and
  briefly again on return after long absence.
- Every allusion carries its own explanation in the same sentence; where the
  frame-voice would compress by allusion, zoom out further and compress by plain
  summary instead. In-jokes for readers of the original are welcome only as a
  bonus layer, never as the load-bearing text.
- Every callback briefly restates what it calls back to.

## Prose and choice discipline

- Default node: **80–150 words** in the frame-voice; hard cap 150 (excluding
  choices and set pieces). Roughly one node in four or five is a **short node,
  under 30 words** — a dated line, one sentence, a fragment. Short nodes still
  carry a choice.
- **First choice on the first screen.** Compress the opening; reach the first
  decision immediately.
- **Two or three sanctioned set pieces** (beyond the mandatory endgame) where a
  full passage of the original may run — choose the novel's most famous moments.
  But pacing: no heavy set piece in the game's first two or three screens. A set
  piece earns its length only after the player has learned what brevity feels
  like; if the novel's great scene comes early, trim it — enter the passage late
  and leave it early.
- **A choice at every node. No pure "continue" buttons** — with one deliberate
  exception: inside the canon endgame, where agency is dying, converging
  no-effect choice-pairs must NOT be dressed up as decisions. Use a single bare
  option there — "(the letter continues)", "(the chapter turns)" — the visible
  death of choice is the mechanism, and the contrast with the choice-rich middle
  game is the point. Most nodes offer 2–4
  options. Total choice count should be high. **Converging choices are
  encouraged**: options leading to the same node
  that differ only in a mark, a tint, or nothing but flavour. Trivial forks and
  fatal forks must be indistinguishable on the surface — the protagonist cannot
  tell them apart either; that is the theme. Delayed consequences via flags/marks
  are welcome.
- **Choice-label legibility.** Every label must be intelligible entirely on its
  own: name the object of the action explicitly; no pronoun whose antecedent is
  not in the same sentence; if the node's final paragraph has moved on to topic
  X, a choice about earlier topic Y must reintroduce Y by name. Literary
  allusion in a label is welcome only on top of a legible action, never instead
  of one.
- Choices are written in the protagonist's idiom — things they might do or say —
  never in game-mechanical language.
- This is a retelling: invent freely on the untrodden paths. Fidelity to tone
  everywhere, fidelity to wording nowhere; the odd original quotation woven in
  where it lands is welcome (the text is public domain).

## Series liturgy (uniform across volumes)

- Title page: "a game of letters, after —" / "a game of chapters, after —" (match
  the novel's unit), author and translator credited, then the frame-voice's preface
  ending with a version of: *the reader of a book follows; the reader of this book
  chooses.*
- FINIS block: ending name in small caps, one verdict line, fidelity accounting,
  where-you-left-the-path list, "begin again."
- Single self-contained HTML file. **Visual design is native to the novel, not to
  the series.** The uniform elements are the grammar only — title formula, FINIS
  block, verdict rhetoric, fidelity accounting. Everything visual (typography,
  colour, texture, layout, motion) must be built from the novel's own time, place,
  and genre temperature: an 18th-century epistolary novel gets foxed paper and a
  Fell face; a 1920s novel gets its own century's print culture; a thriller gets
  visual thrill — pace, contrast, loud colour where loud colour is true. Do not
  default to antiquarian good taste; default to the book. Propose the design
  direction in Stage 1 in one or two sentences alongside the frame-voice sample —
  the two must be of the same mettle.
- GoatCounter in <head>:
  `<script data-goatcounter="https://steffenhuck.goatcounter.com/count" async src="https://gc.zgo.at/count.js"></script>`
  **Page-load count only — do NOT attach event counts to nodes, choices, or any
  in-game action.** One count per visit, on the landing page; nothing else.
- Footer: lowercase italic "steffen huck" linking to https://steffenhuck.github.io
- **Repo and deployment workflow.** The repo already exists and is already
  configured: GitHub Pages deploys automatically from branch `main`, root
  directory. The novel text is already committed. Work directly in this repo:
  put `index.html` (and any assets) at the repo root, and **commit and push
  directly to `main` — do not create branches, pull requests, or subfolders,
  and do not require any merge or deployment step from Steffen.** Every push to
  `main` goes live on its own.

## Process — two stages, approval gate between them

**Stage 1 — read and propose. No passage-writing, no UI.**
After reading `novel.txt` in full, propose:
- the edition/translation identified, and three sentences of frame-voice sample
  prose demonstrating the mettle;
- the forward arrow, and the novel's formal signature (the thing that floods back
  as agency is lost);
- the node map: spine nodes with arrow-positions, fork points, branch routes and
  their rejoin/termination points, including the escape corridors (corridor
  premises are listed for information only — they are your free invention and
  not subject to curation);
- the flag set (≤5), the gates, the marks in outline;
- the ending set (5–6) with trigger conditions, naming the canon slot and the
  genre-flip slot;
- the set pieces;
- what is omitted from the novel, stated plainly.

Then run the design checks and report:
1. **Reachability** — no orphan nodes; every ending attainable.
2. **Canon replication** — a player mimicking the protagonist's actual choices
   reproduces the novel's trajectory and ending exactly.
3. **Non-triviality** — escaping the canon path is genuinely possible but not
   trivial; the arrow has gravity.
4. **Join consistency** — for every merge point, verify each incoming path against
   all downstream text; where a path contradicts it (a character never met, an
   event never witnessed), add conditional variants or move the join.
5. **Micro-chronology and causality** — the graph being a DAG does not protect
   the prose inside a node. Audit every node, sentence by sentence: events must
   be narrated in the order they occur; nothing may be referenced, assumed, or
   foreshadowed as done before the text reaches it (e.g. a ball scene must not
   mention handing the lady into the carriage in mid-scene when that happens at
   the end); choice labels must not presuppose outcomes of the choice; conditional
   variant parts must read correctly in every combination of flags and marks that
   can display them. Run this audit again in Stage 2 on the finished prose, not
   only on the plan.

Wait for approval before Stage 2.

**Stage 2 — write and assemble** the single-file HTML.
